Practice / Concept Project

Shopify Product Page Improvement Concept

A practice case study showing how I would review and improve a Shopify product page for clearer purchase flow, stronger trust signals, better mobile usability, and basic speed discipline.

Case Study Focus
CRO Mobile UX Trust Speed Basics
Overview

Project Overview

The goal is to make a product page easier to understand and easier to buy from. This concept looks at hierarchy, CTA placement, product benefits, trust content, mobile spacing, and page weight.

Problem

Common Product Page Issues

  • Slow page caused by heavy images or unnecessary app scripts.
  • Weak CTA visibility, especially after scrolling on mobile.
  • Low trust signals around reviews, shipping, returns, and payment safety.
  • Mobile layout issues that make product benefits hard to scan.

Audit Checklist

What I would check first.

Above the Fold

Product title, price, rating, CTA, and key benefit should be visible quickly.

Mobile Layout

Spacing, image order, CTA access, and paragraph length should support quick scanning.

Trust Signals

Reviews, returns, shipping, secure payment, and guarantee notes should be easy to find.

Speed Basics

Image size, lazy loading, app scripts, and heavy sections should be reviewed.

Before / After Ideas

Simple improvements that reduce buying friction.

Before After
Long description appears before the buying action. CTA, price, rating, and key benefits appear higher in the page.
Reviews and trust details are hidden low on the page. Compact trust badges and review summary sit near the purchase area.
Large product images slow down the first view. Images are compressed and non-critical media is lazy-loaded.
Shipping and return details are hard to find. FAQ and policy notes answer hesitation before checkout.
CRO

CRO Improvements

  • Make the add-to-cart button visually clear and easy to reach.
  • Add trust badges for secure payment, shipping, returns, or guarantees.
  • Bring review summary and rating information closer to the product title.
  • Rewrite product benefits into short scannable bullets.
  • Add FAQ content for sizing, delivery, returns, and product usage.
Performance

Speed Improvements

  • Compress product images before upload and use appropriate image dimensions.
  • Lazy-load below-the-fold images where possible.
  • Review Shopify apps and scripts that are no longer needed.
  • Simplify sections that add page weight without helping buying decisions.
  • Use PageSpeed Insights as a practical checklist, not the only quality signal.
Tools

Tools Used

  • HTML for content structure.
  • CSS for responsive layout and visual hierarchy.
  • Vanilla JavaScript for product page interactions.
  • Shopify Liquid basics for theme customization learning.
  • PageSpeed Insights checklist for performance review practice.
Learning

What I Learned

Product page quality is not only visual design. A useful Shopify product page needs clear hierarchy, visible buying actions, trust-building details, mobile-first spacing, and careful asset loading. This project helped me connect frontend structure with ecommerce conversion thinking.
